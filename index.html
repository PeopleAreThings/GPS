Dr. Gladys West

        

A Pioneer in Mathematics & GPS Technology

    
         
                 
            

Early Life & Education

            

Dr. Gladys West was born in 1930 in Sutherland, Virginia, in a rural farming community. Despite the challenges of racial segregation, she excelled in academics, earning a full scholarship to Virginia State College. She graduated with a degree in mathematics and later obtained a master’s degree, setting the stage for her groundbreaking career.

        
                 
            

Professional Achievements

            

In 1956, Dr. West was hired at the Naval Surface Warfare Center in Dahlgren, Virginia, as one of the first Black women in a scientific role. Her work focused on complex mathematical modeling and early computer programming.

            

She played a crucial role in the development of the Global Positioning System (GPS) by refining calculations for satellite geolocation. Her work on geodetic models, using IBM computers to analyze Earth’s shape, was fundamental to the technology that millions rely on today.

            

In 2018, she was inducted into the U.S. Air Force Hall of Fame, finally gaining widespread recognition for her pioneering contributions.

        
                 
            

Legacy

            

Dr. West’s work continues to inspire future generations, particularly women and minorities in STEM fields. Her contributions remind us that behind every major technological advancement, there are brilliant minds working diligently to push humanity forward.

            
                

"I did not just speak about doing something, I actually did it."

                
Dr. Gladys West
