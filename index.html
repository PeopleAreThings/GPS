Dr. Gladys West

Pioneering Mathematician & GPS Technology Innovator

Early Life & Education

Childhood in Virginia

Born in 1930 in Sutherland, Virginia, Gladys West emerged from a rural farming community during an era of profound racial segregation. Her exceptional intelligence set her apart from an early age.

Academic Excellence

West graduated as valedictorian of her high school class, earning a full scholarship to Virginia State College. She pursued mathematics, breaking barriers in a field dominated by white men.

Professional Achievements

Naval Career

In 1956, became the second African American woman hired at the Naval Surface Warfare Center Dahlgren Division, pioneering computational mathematics.

GPS Development

Developed crucial mathematical models of the Earth's shape, fundamental to the creation of GPS technology using IBM computers.

Recognition

Inducted into the United States Air Force Hall of Fame in 2018, finally receiving widespread recognition for her groundbreaking work.
